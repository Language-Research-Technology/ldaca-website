This presentations showcase the tools created within the Language Data Commons of Australia. The Language Data Commons of Australia (LDaCA) is making nationally significant language data available for research by universities and communities alike, ensuring enduring access through interoperable repositories, advanced analytic tools, and Indigenous-led governance that embeds appropriate community control. This presentation on LDaCA tools was presented at the 2025 Symposium of the ARDC HASS and Indigenous Research Data Commons, held at UTS on Gadigal Land, Sydney, 11 November 2025.

**Presenters:**
- Professor Michael Haugh, University of Queensland
- Robert McLellan, University of Queensland
- Teresa Chan, University of Queensland
- Professor Monika Bednarek, University of Sydney and Director of Sydney Corpus Lab
- Moises Sacal Bonequi, University of Queensland
