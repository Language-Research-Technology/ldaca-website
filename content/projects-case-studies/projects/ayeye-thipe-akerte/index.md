The sounds and sights of Central Australian birds, along with their Arrernte names and short stories about their appearance, habits and cultural meanings, have been republished on a new website. The website features exquisite artwork by Eastern Arrernte artist and writer Therese Ryder. Originally one in a series of bird apps produced by Batchelor Institute, Ayeye Thipe-akerte is the first to be re-released in a format based on principles of long-term safekeeping. Stay tuned as more bird apps are republished through the partnership between LDaCA and Batchelor Institute.
